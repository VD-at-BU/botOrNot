Visual Sort

'Visual Sort' Puzzle

View details Hide details

'Visual Sort' drops eight small icons on the board and gives you two labels, such as BOOKS and TOOLS or FOOD and JEWELRY. Humans instantly see which icons belong where. Bots don’t. Drag each icon into the correct bucket to complete the puzzle.

Each round is generated from a large image library, so the combinations keep changing without any hard-coded questions. The challenge is simple for humans and difficult for automated scripts.

Spot the Odd

'Spot the Odd' Puzzle

View details Hide details

'Spot the Odd' shows eight icons: seven from a single category and one clear imposter. Your goal is to spot the item that doesn’t belong and drag it into the odd-one-out drop zone. Humans are very good at “this just doesn’t fit” judgments; bots struggle when the artwork and categories change every time.

The engine simply chooses two categories and samples icons at random. No subjective attributes are needed; the difference is purely visual and category-based.

Find the Perfect Fit

'Find the Perfect Fit' Puzzle

View details Hide details

'Find the Perfect Fit' flips the 'Spot the Odd' puzzle idea. The board starts filled with icons from a single category, such as fish. From a separate set of candidates, only one icon would blend naturally into that group. Drag the one that fits into the target zone.

The puzzle is defined using only categories. Seven candidates are drawn from the “wrong” groups and one from the matching group. It’s a tiny, visual reasoning test that feels natural to humans and awkward for bots.

What Is BotOrNot?

BotOrNot is a human-friendly verification layer that keeps bots out without punishing real users. Instead of distorted text or unreadable images, BotOrNot uses small drag-and-drop puzzles that feel more like a mini-game than a security gate.

The system works with a compact icon library, such as books, boots, food, jewelry, tools, fish, flowers, and a few simple rules. That’s enough to generate thousands of unique puzzle instances without pre-written questions.

How BotOrNot Works

When a visitor reaches your verification step, BotOrNot creates a fresh puzzle on the fly by randomly selecting categories and icons from the image library. The user solves a short drag-and-drop task that leverages natural visual reasoning: “these all match, that one doesn’t”.

Automated scripts can read text and follow fixed flows, but they’re much weaker at quickly understanding visual groupings. BotOrNot leans into that gap to raise the cost of scripted abuse while keeping the experience obvious and quick for humans.
